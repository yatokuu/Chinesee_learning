Chinese Culture & Traditions

Language lives inside culture — explore both

🎆

Chinese New Year

春节 · Chūnjié

The biggest celebration of the year. Families reunite, fireworks light the sky, and red envelopes (红包 hóngbāo) carry good luck for 15 magical days.

Festival
🀄

Chinese Characters

汉字 · Hànzì

Over 3,000 years old — each character is a tiny painting of meaning. Just 3,500 characters cover 99% of modern Chinese text.

Language
🍵

Tea Culture

茶文化 · Chá wénhuà

Tea (茶 chá) has been central to Chinese life for 5,000 years. The gongfu tea ceremony is a meditation — slow, intentional, and beautiful.

Tradition
🐉

The Dragon Symbol

龙 · Lóng

The Chinese dragon is a benevolent guardian of luck, wisdom, and power — the symbol of imperial nobility and national pride.

Mythology
🥢

Dining Etiquette

饮食礼仪 · Yǐnshí lǐyí

Never stick chopsticks upright in rice. Pour tea for elders first. Say 干杯 (Gānbēi) when toasting. Food is how love is expressed in China.

Etiquette
🌙

Mid-Autumn Festival

中秋节 · Zhōngqiūjié

Under the full moon, families share mooncakes (月饼 yuèbǐng) and tell the legend of Chang'e, the moon goddess who flew to the moon.
